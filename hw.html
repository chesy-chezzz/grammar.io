📚 Present Simple vs Present Continuous

✅ positive · ❌ negative · ❓ interrogative + structures & rules
🔹 Present Simple: facts / habits / routines ⏳ Present Continuous: now / around now / temporary

📘 Present Simple

➕ POSITIVE
I / You / We / They → verb
He / She / It → verb + -s / -es
✨ I walk my dog. / She feeds the cat. / He plays with a parrot.
➖ NEGATIVE
I / You / We / They → do not (don't) + verb
He / She / It → does not (doesn't) + verb
🚫 They don't like snakes. / He doesn't clean the cage.
❓ QUESTION
Do / Does + subject + verb?
❓ Do you have a rabbit? / Does your parrot talk?
📆 when? every day, usually, always, sometimes, on Mondays, never
💡 tip Stative verbs (love, hate, know, understand) → only Present Simple

⏳ Present Continuous

➕ POSITIVE
am / is / are + verb-ing
🐕 I am walking the dog now. / She is feeding the rabbit.
➖ NEGATIVE
am not / isn't / aren't + verb-ing
😴 The cat isn't sleeping right now. / We aren't playing.
❓ QUESTION
Am / Is / Are + subject + verb-ing?
🐹 Is your hamster running? / Are they barking?
⏰ now markers now, right now, at the moment, today, this week, Look! / Listen!
🎯 special use temporary actions, changing situations, annoying habits (with "always")

🔍 Compare: I work (every day) vs I am working (right now).  |  She doesn't eat meat (fact) vs She isn't eating meat today (temporary).
